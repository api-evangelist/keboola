When companies acquire new entities, their chart of accounts typically breaks due to structural incompatibilities between systems. The article identifies five failure modes (knowledge silos, repeated mapping work, accounting standard conflicts, hidden intercompany issues, and auditability gaps) that compound over time and are best solved through a governed mapping layer rather than manual spreadsheets.
